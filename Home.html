Typhoon Preparedness Guide

Typhoon preparedness is essential for saving lives, protecting property, ensuring the availability of basic necessities, and reducing the long-term impact on communities and economies. By taking the time to plan and prepare, individuals and communities are better equipped to respond effectively and recover swiftly when a typhoon strikes.

Before the Typhoon:

  • Stay informed by monitoring weather updates and warnings.
  • Create an emergency kit with water, food, flashlights, first aid, and important documents.
  • Prepare your home by securing windows, doors, and outdoor objects.
  • Make an evacuation plan and pack a go-bag with essentials.
  • Ensure pets are safe and have food and identification tags.

During the Typhoon:

  • Stay indoors, away from windows and glass doors.
  • Avoid using electrical appliances or touching water during heavy rain.
  • Continue listening to weather reports for updates.
  • If instructed, evacuate early, avoiding flooded areas.
  • Do not go outside until authorities declare it safe.

After the Typhoon:

  • Wait for official confirmation that it is safe to go outside.
  • Inspect your home for damage and check for gas leaks.
  • Stay away from floodwaters and debris.
  • Check on neighbors, especially elderly or vulnerable individuals.
  • Document damage for insurance and recovery purposes.
  • Limit driving until roads are cleared and safe.
